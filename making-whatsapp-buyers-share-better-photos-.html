How we redesigned the buyer journey to capture quality product images and reduce empty leads

Introduction

Every day, thousands of B2B buyers reach out to sellers on WhatsApp through IndiaMART. They're looking for products, asking for quotes, and trying to explain exactly what they need. But there's a problem: many buyers send messages without any product photos, or worse, they send nothing at all.

These blank or photo-less buying leads (BLs) create friction. Sellers can't understand what the buyer wants. Buyers get frustrated waiting for responses. And the conversation that should lead to a transaction stalls before it even begins.

We call these "blank BLs" - leads where buyers don't share enough information for sellers to respond effectively. On the flip side, when buyers share clear product photos, the conversation flows smoothly. Sellers know exactly what to quote for, and deals close faster.

So we asked ourselves: How do we nudge buyers to share photos during their WhatsApp journey? Not through mandatory fields or blocking their progress, but by making it feel natural and helpful to share an image.

This is the story of how we optimized the WhatsApp buyer journey to increase photo-rich leads and reduce blank ones.

The Problem: Too Many Leads Without Context ðŸš§

When a buyer connects with a seller through WhatsApp, the quality of that first message matters enormously. If a buyer just says "I need this product" without a photo or detailed description, the seller is left guessing.

Our data showed a troubling pattern:

  • High percentage of blank BLs: Many conversations started with little to no product information
  • Low photo attachment rates: Buyers weren't naturally sharing product images
  • Seller frustration: Sellers had to ask follow-up questions just to understand basic requirements
  • Longer response times: Without context, sellers took longer to reply or didn't reply at all

The WhatsApp journey wasn't encouraging buyers to share photos at the right moment. The flow moved too quickly from interest to message, skipping the crucial step of gathering product details.

We needed to redesign the journey to make photo sharing feel like a natural, helpful part of reaching out to a seller.

The Solution: Nudging at the Right Moment âœ¨

We rebuilt the WhatsApp buyer journey with one core principle: make it easy and obvious to share a photo before sending the message.

Understanding the New Flow

The optimized journey introduces strategic touchpoints where buyers are prompted to add product photos. Instead of rushing straight to "send message," we added gentle nudges that highlight the value of sharing an image.

Key Design Choices

We made several intentional decisions to optimize the flow:

  • Visual prominence: Photo upload options are displayed clearly, not hidden in menus
  • Value messaging: We explain why sharing a photo helps ("Get accurate quotes faster")
  • Non-blocking: Buyers can still proceed without a photo, but the default path encourages it
  • Mobile-first: The flow is optimized for smartphone users, IndiaMART's primary audience

Testing and Iteration

We didn't get it right on the first try. Early versions were too pushy, making buyers feel forced to upload photos. Others were too subtle, and buyers missed the option entirely.

Through A/B testing, we found the sweet spot: contextual prompts that feel helpful, not mandatory. We tested different copy, button placements, and visual hierarchies to find what worked best.

Why This Matters for B2B Marketplaces ðŸ’¡

The WhatsApp buyer journey might seem like a small UX problem, but it has massive implications for B2B marketplaces.

Lead quality drives everything. In B2B, especially in India's diverse manufacturing and trading ecosystem, a single photo can convey more information than paragraphs of text. A buyer looking for industrial valves can show exactly what size, material, and configuration they need with one image.

Reducing friction accelerates transactions. When buyers share photos upfront, sellers can respond with accurate quotes immediately. This cuts out days of back-and-forth clarification and moves deals forward faster.

Better experience for both sides. Buyers get relevant responses. Sellers waste less time on unclear leads. Everyone wins when communication starts with clear information.

By optimizing this journey, we're not just improving a flow, we're making the entire marketplace more efficient.

Conclusion

Blank buying leads were a silent problem that affected thousands of buyer-seller conversations every day. By redesigning the WhatsApp journey to encourage photo sharing at the right moment, we've made it easier for buyers to express their needs and for sellers to respond effectively.

The solution wasn't about adding more steps or mandatory fields. It was about understanding the buyer's mindset and designing a flow that naturally guides them toward the best outcome. A simple nudge, placed at the right time, can transform lead quality.

This optimization is now live and helping thousands of B2B transactions start on the right foot. As we continue to monitor the data, we'll keep refining the journey to make it even more seamless.

For product teams working on B2B platforms or messaging-based commerce, the lesson is clear: don't just move users through a flow, guide them toward actions that help everyone. The best UX doesn't just serve the user, it serves the entire ecosystem.
